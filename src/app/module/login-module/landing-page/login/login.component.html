Privacy Policy for Website and on the YQMS Farmer Information System ("OFIS")

  1. This privacy policy (" Policy") sets out how YQMS International Limited (" YQMS") collects, uses and discloses personal data which Farmers, Customers, Employees, Suppliers and other third parties give to YQMS via their website or the OFIS.
  2. Introduction
    1. YQMS requires all Farmers, Customers, Employees, Suppliers and other third parties to handle personal data of Farmers, Customers, Employees, Suppliers and other third parties in a way that is consistent with YQMS's obligations under applicable law which can be summarized as follows: YQMS must notify individuals of the purposes for which it intends to collect, use or disclose the individuals' personal data on or before such collection, use or disclosure of personal data.
    2. YQMS may only collect, use or disclose personal data about an individual for purposes that are reasonable in the circumstances and that have been notified to the individual.
    3. YQMS must obtain the consent of individuals before collecting, using or disclosing their personal data. Clear and express consent is required.
    4. YQMS must, upon request, provide individuals with their personal data in YQMS's possession or control as well as information about the ways in which the personal data may have been used or disclosed during the past year.
    5. YQMS must, upon request, correct an error or omission in an individual's personal data that is in YQMS's possession or control.
    6. YQMS must make a reasonable effort to ensure that personal data collected by or on behalf of it is accurate and complete.
    7. YQMS must protect the confidentiality, integrity and availability of personal data in its possession or control by making reasonable security arrangements to prevent unauthorized access, collection, use, disclosure, copying, modification, disposal or similar risks.
    8. YQMS should only retain personal data for as long as it is necessary to fulfil the purposes for which the personal data was collected or as necessary for other legal or business reasons.
    9. YQMS must cease to retain personal data or remove the means by which the personal data can be associated with particular individuals as soon as it is reasonable to assume that the purpose for which the personal data was collected is no longer being served by retention of the personal data and retention is no longer necessary for YQMS's legal or business purposes.
    10. YQMS may transfer personal data to a country or jurisdiction out of the applicable jurisdiction if it has taken appropriate steps to: (1) ensure that it will comply with applicable law, while such data remains in its possession or under its control and (2) to ascertain whether and to ensure that the recipient is bound by legally enforceable obligations to provide the transferred personal data a standard of protection that is at least comparable to the protection under applicable law.
    11. YQMS must implement necessary policies and procedures to meet its obligations under applicable law where necessary, such as the appointment of a data protection officer and make available his/her contact particulars to the individuals, effective staff communication and making publicly available information about such policies and procedures,
    12. If there is a failure in the protection of the personal data it manages, YQMS must shall notify in writing to the owner of such personal data, if the owner resides in Indonesia, or if the personal data was collected in Indonesia.

    This Policy will be reviewed and amended on an annual basis and as and when required to accommodate any changes to applicable law or better practices in relation to the protection of Farmers, Customers, Employees, Suppliers and other third parties' personal data.

    References in this Policy to "Farmers, Customers, Employees, Suppliers and other third parties" refer to any past, present or potential farmers, customers, employees, suppliers and other third parties of YQMS. References in this Policy to "YQMS" refers to YQMS Singapore Pte Ltd, YQMS South East Asia Pte Ltd and its subsidiaries and affiliates.

  3. What is "Personal Data"?

    This Policy applies to the personal data of Farmers, Customers, Employees, Suppliers and other third parties. "Personal Data" means data, whether true or not, about an individual who can be identified (a) from that data; or (b) from that data and other information to which the organisation has or is likely to have access.

    Examples of personal data include a Farmer's name, an Employee's staff code, identification number, personal contact details and any generic information which can be used to identify an individual when associated with personal data.

    Business contact information is not considered to be personal data unless they are provided solely for personal purposes. For example, the name, position, work address, work telephone number, and work email address on an individual's business card are usually not personal data. However, there are some circumstances in which the individual provided work related personal data solely for personal purposes.

  4. Collection of Personal Data - Notification, Consent and Withdrawal of Consent

    Notification and Consent

    1. Before or at the time of collection, use or disclosure of personal data, the purposes of collection, use or disclosure of the personal data must be notified to the individual whose personal data YQMS intends to collect, use or disclose. Such purposes must be reasonable in the circumstances.
    2. When collecting personal data through a form, YQMS should indicate which fields that collect personal data are compulsory and which are optional. YQMS should avoid collecting more information than is necessary from Farmers, Customers, Employees, Suppliers and other third parties. The data collected should be reasonable, adequate and not excessive in relation to that purpose.
    3. When collecting, using or disclosing an individual's personal data is necessary for the providing of services, YQMS must require the individual to give consent to the collection, use or disclosure of his personal data and make such consent a condition of providing of services by YQMS to the individual. When collecting, using or disclosing an individual's personal data for the purposes beyond what is reasonable for providing services to the individual, YQMS should obtain the individual's consent for such purposes, and must not make the consent a condition of providing services.
    4. YQMS must seek to obtain the consent of individuals to the collection, use and disclosure of their personal data for the notified purposes. YQMS should therefore obtain consent in writing, via email, via online platform or at least record the consent in a manner that is accessible for future reference by noting the fact that oral consent was provided by an individual for certain purposes, together with the date and time of such consent. YQMS should then, to the extent possible, confirm in writing the verbal consent given with the individual.
    5. In the absence of clear and express consent, however, consent may be deemed by the voluntary submission of personal data following adequate notification of reasonable purposes. YQMS should therefore ensure that the notifications have been sufficiently brought to the attention of the individuals.
    6. If YQMS wishes to use or disclose personal data for purposes which it has not yet informed the relevant individual or for which it has not yet obtained consent, YQMS must inform the individual of the new purposes and obtain fresh consent.
    7. When YQMS collects personal data from a third party source, it must notify and provide sufficient information to the source of the purposes for which YQMS intends to collect, use and disclose the personal data. YQMS should also exercise appropriate due diligence to check and ensure, or request self-certification from the third party that the third party source has already obtained consent for disclosure of the personal data to YQMS. YQMS should:
      • seek a contractual undertaking or at least obtain written confirmation from the third party source that the disclosure of personal data to YQMS is within the scope of the consent given by the relevant individual to the third party source; or
      • obtain a copy of the document(s) containing or evidencing the consent given by the relevant individual to the third party source to disclose personal data to YQMS.
    8. During the first time of contacting individuals whose personal data has been provided by a third party source, YQMS should, if asked by the individual, inform the individual that their personal data was disclosed to YQMS by the third party source and verify that the individual has provided the third party source consent to do so.

    Withdrawal of Consent

    1. YQMS must allow an individual who has previously given (or is deemed to have given) consent for the collection, use or disclosure of personal data for stated purposes to withdraw such consent by giving reasonable notice. An individual may communicate objection to YQMS's continual use and/or disclosure of personal data for stated purposes by contacting YQMS at OFIS@YQMSnet.com
    2. Upon receipt of a withdrawal of consent, YQMS must cease, and cause all data intermediaries and agents (if applicable) to cease collecting, using or disclosing the personal data for the purposes and in the manner objected to. YQMS must also inform the relevant individual of the consequences of withdrawing the consent upon receipt of a withdrawal request e.g. inability to process an account registration or inability to further progress a job application.

    Personal Data Which may be collected and used on OFIS

    1. Personal data which may be collected from Farmers and their families may include their name, date of birth, education leave, bank account details, address, migration, nationality, marital status, number of children and children's date of birth.
    2. This personal data may be used on OFIS by Employees of YQMS, farmer group management staff and Customers (" Users") on mapping software designed to showcase farm and infrastructure locations. The Users of OFIS have the option on the mapping software to look at specific farmer details based on location. Users are able to export analysis (and data behind that analysis, including the Farmers personal data) into an excel spread-sheet or PDF to identify specific Farmers, and analyse the personal data alongside statistics such as productivity levels.
    3. Farm management plans are pulled together using personal data on OFIS. These are tailor made plans for each individual farmer highlighting the changes that need to be made over the next 3-5 years in order that the Farmer reaches his full productivity potential. These will be printed by either an employee of YQMS or a member of the Farmer Group, and distributed to the relevant Farmer.

    Personal Data which may be collected on the YQMS Website

    1. Personal data which may be collected on the YQMS website includes name and contact information including email address and postcodes.
    2. This personal data will be used by YQMS employees to answer enquiries sent via the "Contact Us" section of the YQMS Website. The personal data may be retained by YQMS employees to assist in the administering of the relationship between YQMS and the enquiring third party.
  5. Use and Disclosure of Personal Data - Use of Data Intermediaries and Transfer of personal data

    Use of Data Intermediaries

    1. YQMS may from time to time engage third party organizations, including affiliated companies, to process personal data on behalf of YQMS. Data Intermediaries are under an obligation to:
      • protect personal data disclosed to it by YQMS from unauthorized access, collection, use, disclosure, copying, modification, disposal or similar risks; and
      • only keep the personal data for as long as the data is required for the business and legal purposes of the data intermediaries.
    2. Before appointing Data Intermediaries, YQMS should use reasonable efforts to undertake an appropriate level of due diligence to assure itself that a potential data intermediary is capable of complying with its obligations under this Policy and applicable law.
    3. When appointing data intermediaries, YQMS should include provisions in the written contracts to clearly set out the data intermediaries' responsibilities and liabilities in relation to protection and retention of personal data.
    4. Notwithstanding the appointment of data intermediaries, YQMS remains bound to comply with this Policy and applicable law in full with respect to personal data that is processed by data intermediaries on its behalf.

    Transfer of personal data outside the relevant jurisdiction

    1. Before transferring personal data overseas, YQMS should contractually require the recipient to provide the personal data transferred a standard of protection that is at least comparable to the protection under applicable law and limit the countries and territories to which personal data may be further transferred under the contract. Such a contract should at least set out obligations in respect of the personal data transferred, with regard to:
      • where the recipient is a data intermediary of YQMS,
        • protection; and
        • retention limitation;
      • where the recipient is an organization (other than a data intermediary of YQMS).
        • Purpose of collection, use and disclosure by the recipient;
        • Accuracy;
        • Protection;
        • Retention limitation;
        • Policies on personal data protection; and
        • Access and correction of individuals.
    2. If it is impractical to achieve any of the above, YQMS should ensure that:
      • the transfer of personal data is necessary for the performance of a contract between YQMS and the individual concerned or necessary to do anything at the individual's request with a view to his entering a contract with YQMS;
      • the transfer of personal data is necessary for the conclusion or performance of a contract between YQMS and a third party which is entered into at the individual's request, or which it is reasonable to assume to be in the individual's interest;
      • the transfer of personal data is necessary to respond to an emergency that threatens the life, health or safety of an individual, in which case YQMS may only transfer personal data if it has taken reasonable steps to ensure that he personal data will not be used or disclosed by the recipient for any other purpose.
  6. Access and Correction Requests
    1. Upon request by an individual, YQMS will provide the individual with the following as soon as reasonably possible:
      • personal data about the individual that is in YQMS's possession or control;
      • information about the ways in which that personal data has been or may have been used or disclosed by YQMS within the past year. (For this purpose, the DPO should retain a list of all possible third parties to whom personal data may have been disclosed by YQMS. YQMS should individually identify each possible third party to whom YQMS has disclosed personal data, rather than simply providing general categories of organizations. This would allow individuals to directly approach the third party organizations to which personal data has been disclosed.)
    2. In responding to a data access request, YQMS is not required to provide access to materials that do not comprise or contain the personal data requested. YQMS is also not required to provide access to information which is no longer in its possession or control when the access request is received, in which case YQMS should so inform the requesting individual.
    3. Where personal data is obtained from a third party source, YQMS is not required to provide information on the source to the requesting individual.
    4. YQMS may charge the individual a reasonable fee for producing a copy of the materials containing the personal data requested.
    5. If the personal data resides in a form that cannot practicably be provided to the individual in documentary form, whether as physical or electronic copies, then YQMS may provide the individual a reasonable opportunity to examine the requested data.
    6. Before responding to an access request, YQMS should exercise due diligence and adopt appropriate measures to verify the individual's identity to ensure that the personal data being requested actually relates to the requesting individual.
    7. YQMS is not required to provide access to personal data if:
      • the request would unreasonably interfere with the operations of YQMS because of the repetitious or systematic nature of the request;
      • the burden or expense of providing access would be unreasonable to YQMS or disproportionate to the individual's interest;
      • the request is for information that is trivial; or
      • the access request is frivolous or vexatious.
    8. Upon request by an individual to correct personal data held by YQMS, YQMS should first consider whether, in the circumstances, the correction should be made. Unless YQMS is satisfied on reasonable grounds that the correction should not be made, it should as soon as practicable:
      • correct the personal data as soon as practicable; and
      • send the corrected personal data to every other organization (or only to specific organizations if the individual concerned has consented) to which the personal data was disclosed by YQMS within the past year unless that other organization does not need the corrected personal data for any legal or business purposes.
    9. YQMS may not impose a charge for the correction of personal data upon request.
    10. If YQMS is satisfied on reasonable grounds that the correction should not be made, it should annotate the reasons why it has decided that the correction should not be made.
    11. In any event, YQMS should respond to an access or correction request as soon as possible. If YQMS is unable to respond to a request within 30 days of the request, it should inform the requesting individual in writing within 30 days of receipt of the request the expected time frame for YQMS to be able to respond to the request along with the reasons why YQMS is unable to respond within 30 days.
  7. Care of Personal Data - Ensuring accuracy of data, Security Measures and Retention Policies

    Ensuring accuracy of personal data

    1. YQMS should ensure that the personal data held by it is accurate and complete. YQMS requests that individuals only provide to YQMS personal data which is accurate and complete and not misleading and that individuals should keep such data up to date and inform YQMS of any changes.
    2. Where the currency of personal data is important for a specific reason, YQMS should verify that the personal data provided by the individual concerned is up to date e.g. by requesting the individual to confirm their personal data before using or disclosing the data.
    3. Where personal data has been obtained from a third party source, YQMS should obtain confirmation by way of a self-certification from the source that the source has verified the accuracy and completeness of the personal data provided to YQMS.

    Security Measures

    1. Reasonable security standards should be upheld by all Staff of YQMS and Users of OFIS, when dealing with personal data.

    Retention of personal data

    1. YQMS must cease to retain documents containing personal data as soon as it is reasonable to assume that the purpose for which the personal data was collected is no longer being served by retention of the personal data and retention is no longer necessary for YQMS's legal or business purposes.
    2. YQMS should not keep personal data "just in case" the data may be required for purposes that have not been notified to the individual concerned.
    3. Once it is determined that personal data is no longer required, YQMS should:
      • destroy the documents containing the personal data (physical files and documents containing personal data must not be thrown into general rubbish bins. They should be shredded or placed into confidential waste bins/always dispose of information media (including diskette, back-up tapes, CDs, DVDs, USB Memory, old IT equipment, etc.) containing personal data using confidential waste bins.); OR
      • effectively anonymise the personal data.
    4. Please note that the following measures are not sufficient to cease retention of personal data:
      • filing documents away in a locked cabinet;
      • warehousing documents;
      • archiving electronic copies of documents;
      • merely limiting access to electronic copies of documents.
